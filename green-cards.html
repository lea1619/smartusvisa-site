Employment-Based Green Cards

Permanent residency through extraordinary ability, national interest, and investment.

EB-1A — Extraordinary Ability Green Card

Top pick for high achievers: EB-1A requires no employer sponsor, no PERM labor certification, and falls in the highest employment-based priority category, meaning shorter wait times for most nationalities.

10 USCIS Criteria (must meet 3+)

  • Receipt of lesser nationally/internationally recognized prizes or awards
  • Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
  • Published material about you in professional or major trade publications
  • Participation as a judge of the work of others in the field
  • Original scientific, scholarly, artistic, athletic, or business-related contributions of major significance
  • Authorship of scholarly articles in professional publications
  • Artistic exhibitions or showcases (for arts)
  • Critical or essential role for distinguished organizations
  • High salary or remuneration relative to peers
  • Commercial successes in performing arts

Process

  1. 1

    Self-Petition I-140

    File Form I-140 with evidence package demonstrating extraordinary ability.

  2. 2

    Priority Date Becomes Current

    Wait for your priority date to be current in the monthly Visa Bulletin.

  3. 3

    I-485 Adjustment of Status or Consular Processing

    File I-485 if in the U.S., or attend immigrant visa interview abroad.

  4. 4

    Green Card Received

    Receive permanent resident card. Conditions apply for certain cases.

EB-2 NIW — National Interest Waiver

Most popular self-petition for professionals. EB-2 NIW requires no employer and no PERM. Under the Matter of Dhanasar standard (2016), USCIS evaluates three prongs: substantial merit, national importance, and on-balance benefit to the U.S.

Three-Prong Dhanasar Test

  1. Substantial Merit and National Importance: Your endeavor must have substantial intrinsic merit and be national in scope (not just local).
  2. Well-Positioned to Advance the Endeavor: Demonstrate education, skills, knowledge, or record of success to advance the proposed endeavor.
  3. On Balance, Beneficial to Waive Job Offer/PERM: Show that waiving the job offer requirement benefits the U.S. national interest.

Popular Qualifying Fields

  • STEM Research & Academia
  • Technology / AI / Software Engineering
  • Healthcare & Medical Research
  • Business / Finance / Entrepreneurship
  • Public Policy / Law
  • Environmental Science & Clean Energy

India & China Priority Date Note

Priority date backlog: India and China EB-2 priority dates are significantly backlogged (as of 2025, India EB-2 may be 5-10+ years). However, EB-1 India moves faster. Many Indian nationals pursue EB-1A as a faster alternative. Check the USCIS Visa Bulletin monthly.

Required Documentation

  • Academic credentials (degree, transcripts)
  • Publications, citations, or research impact
  • Letters from experts in your field
  • Evidence of national importance (grants, media, impact)
  • Proposed endeavor statement

EB-1C — Multinational Manager/Executive

EB-1C is for multinational managers or executives who have been employed abroad and are transferring to a U.S. parent, subsidiary, or affiliate. It is the natural pathway from an L-1A visa. No PERM required; falls in EB-1 priority category.

Requirements

  • Employed as manager or executive for at least 1 of last 3 years by foreign entity
  • U.S. employer must be related to foreign employer (parent, sub, affiliate, branch)
  • Petitioner must be coming to manage or execute (not staff work)
  • U.S. employer must have been doing business for at least 1 year

L-1A to EB-1C Pathway

The most common path: come to the U.S. on L-1A, establish and grow the U.S. entity, then file EB-1C after 1 year of U.S. business operations. No PERM required, EB-1 priority.

EB-5 — Immigrant Investor

The EB-5 visa allows foreign nationals to obtain a green card by investing in a U.S. commercial enterprise and creating at least 10 full-time jobs for U.S. workers.

Investment Requirements (2025)

  • $1,050,000 minimum investment (standard)
  • $800,000 in Targeted Employment Areas (TEA) or Regional Centers
  • Must create or preserve at least 10 full-time jobs
  • Investment must be "at risk"

Direct vs. Regional Center

Direct EB-5: Invest directly in a new commercial enterprise you manage.

Regional Center: Passive investment in USCIS-designated regional centers. Jobs can be direct or indirect (counted by economic models). More flexible.
