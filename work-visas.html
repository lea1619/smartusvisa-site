U.S. Work Visas

Detailed guides to H-1B, O-1A, L-1, TN, and other employment-based nonimmigrant visas.

H-1B Visa — Specialty Occupation

FY2026 H-1B Key Dates: Cap-subject H-1B registration was March 2025. New H-1B status starts October 1, 2025. FY2027 registration expected March 2026.

Eligibility

  • Specialty occupation requiring at least a bachelor's degree
  • U.S. employer willing to sponsor and file the petition
  • Employer must pay prevailing wage
  • Employer-employee relationship must exist

Key Features

  • Dual intent — can pursue green card while on H-1B
  • Initial 3-year period, extendable to 6 years
  • Cap-exempt: universities, nonprofits, gov research
  • Premium processing available (~2-5 business days)

H-1B Process

  1. 1

    LCA Filing

    Employer files Labor Condition Application with DOL (7 business days).

  2. 2

    Lottery Registration (cap-subject)

    USCIS opens registration each March. Lottery randomly selects petitioners.

  3. 3

    I-129 Petition

    Employer files I-129. Standard: 3-6 months. Premium: ~5 days.

  4. 4

    Visa/Status

    Consular stamping (if abroad) or change of status (if in U.S.).

The H-1B has an annual cap of 65,000 visas plus 20,000 additional for U.S. master's or higher degree holders. Cap-exempt employers (universities, nonprofits, government research) are not subject to the cap and may file any time.

Yes. Under H-1B portability (AC21), you can change employers if you have an I-140 approval and pending H-1B extension. New employer files an H-1B transfer petition.

Alternatives include O-1A (no cap), TN visa (for Canadians/Mexicans), L-1 (intracompany), cap-exempt employers, or E-3 (for Australians).

O-1A Visa — Extraordinary Ability

No lottery, no cap, no annual wait. O-1A can be filed at any time of year. No strict employer required — a U.S. agent can file on your behalf.

Evidence Criteria (need 3+ of these)

  • Nationally/internationally recognized prizes or awards
  • Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
  • Published material about you in major trade publications or media
  • Judging the work of others in your field
  • Original contributions of major significance
  • Authorship of scholarly articles
  • Critical role for distinguished organizations
  • High salary relative to peers

Yes. O-1A is the strongest bridge to EB-1A (Extraordinary Ability Green Card). Meeting O-1A criteria often aligns closely with EB-1A requirements.

L-1 Visa — Intracompany Transferee

L-1A (Managers/Executives) vs L-1B (Specialized Knowledge)

L-1A: For managers who manage a function, department, or organization, or executives who direct organization activities. Maximum 7 years. Leads to EB-1C green card.

L-1B: For employees with specialized knowledge of the company's products, procedures, techniques, or proprietary systems. Maximum 5 years.

Requirements

  • Employed by foreign entity for at least 1 of last 3 years
  • U.S. and foreign entities must be related (parent, sub, affiliate, branch)
  • Qualifying managerial, executive, or specialized knowledge role

Key Benefits

  • No annual cap or lottery
  • Dual intent allowed — can pursue green card
  • Blanket L petitions for qualifying multinationals
  • L-1A is direct path to EB-1C green card (no PERM)
  • New office petitions for establishing a U.S. entity

Yes. L-1 new office petitions allow executives/managers to establish a new U.S. entity. Initial period is 1 year; extensions as the business grows.

TN Visa — USMCA Professional

Canadians only: TN status can be obtained same-day at a U.S. port of entry with a job offer letter and required documents. No advance filing with USCIS required.

Qualifying USMCA Professions (selected)

  • Engineers (all disciplines)
  • Computer Systems Analysts
  • Scientists (Biologist, Chemist, etc.)
  • Accountant (CPA)
  • Lawyer (LLB or JD)
  • Pharmacist
  • Physician (teaching/research only)
  • Graphic Designer
  • Management Consultant

TN vs H-1B

  • No annual cap or lottery
  • 3-year increments, renewable indefinitely
  • Canadians: same-day at port of entry
  • Mexicans: must apply at U.S. consulate (2-8 weeks)
  • No dual intent — cannot directly pursue green card
  • Low filing fees vs H-1B

Note: While TN does not have dual intent, TN holders may pursue green cards through other means (e.g., EB-1A, EB-2 NIW) while maintaining TN status.

Yes. Canadian citizens can present to CBP at a U.S. land border or airport with a job offer letter, proof of qualifications, and $56 fee to obtain TN status on the spot.
